About Max

After studying history and music, Max took a circuitous route into the legal profession and to the north east of England. He is a partner at the Newcastle firm Sintons LLP and practices nationally in the fields of employment and discrimination. He is often quoted in the media on matters related to employment law and litigation and has appeared on both national radio and television. In 2017 he was appointed the Chair of the Law Society’s Employment Law Committee. He is married and lives in Northumberland.

About the blog

Having been introduced to the works of Ludwig Wittgenstein by an employment judge during a submission on implied terms, Max has always believed that lawyer should be more open to ideas outside their apparent discipline. Unfortunately, in these austere times, the opportunities for such discourse are becoming increasing rare, and whilst looking forward to the possibility that future cases may involve statutes older than the Act of Annates of 1532 or more obscure than the Cemetery Clauses Act of 1847, this blog is intended to cover aspects of knowledge that wouldn’t normally feature in skeleton arguments in the employment tribunal.
